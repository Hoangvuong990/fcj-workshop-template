### General Information
* **Event Name:** Cloud Architect Game Show
* **Date & Time:** June 20, 2026
* **Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City
* **Role:** Attendee

### Main Activities & Highlights

**1. Gamified Learning Experience**
To foster a lively learning environment and encourage collaboration, the program introduced the "Cloud Architect" game show. Departing from standard seminar formats, 8 teams competed directly, solving a series of cloud computing and infrastructure design challenges that increased in difficulty.

**2. Team Formation & Networking**
The competition served as an excellent networking platform. Rather than sticking to our regular study groups, we were free to form 5-person teams with participants from other cohorts. The single rule to maintain a level playing field was that no experienced cloud experts could assist the teams.

**3. Strategic Gameplay & Lifelines**
The game show was as much about risk management strategy as it was about AWS technical knowledge. Teams were provided with two distinct, one-time lifelines:
*   **Risk Mitigation (Rủi ro tối thiểu):** An option for uncertain questions. An incorrect answer carries no penalty (zero points deducted), but a correct answer yields only half the standard score.
*   **Star of Hope (Ngôi sao hy vọng):** A high-risk, high-reward choice for confident questions. A correct answer doubles the points, whereas a wrong response results in a double penalty.

**4. The Thrill of the Tie-Breaker**
The sudden-death structure kept the energy levels high throughout. The peak of excitement arrived with the tie-breaker "Question 11." When teams ended regular rounds with equal scores, this sudden-death scenario demanded not only a deep understanding of cloud patterns but also quick reflexes to clinch victory.

### Takeaways & Reflections
This activity was a fantastic stress-reliever after weeks of intense training. Proposing AWS architecture layouts under time constraints and competing with other teams significantly improved my quick-thinking abilities. It showed that studying cloud architecture can be engaging and dynamic when paired with teamwork and a bit of game strategy.
